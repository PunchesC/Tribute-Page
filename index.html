Major Battles

Battle of Alesia

The battle of Alesia was the last major revolt in Gaul, which is the territory know as France today. The Guals were a loose confederation of tribes lead by a man name Vercingetorix. The battle was more of a seige, inwhich Ceasar build double walls to keep Vercingetorix from escaping and any relief forces from attacking the Romans from behhind. Ceasar's victory secured Roman domination of the Gauls for centuries to come. This is considered one of the great battles in history.

  • Date: September 52 B.C.
  • Armies Strength
    • Romans: 60-70,000
    • Gauls: 70-100,000
  • Casualties
    • Romans: 12,800 killed or wounded
    • Gauls: Disputed

Battle of Pharsalus

The Battle of Pharsalus was the decisive battle of Caesar's Civil War. On 9 August 48 BC at Pharsalus in central Greece, Gaius Julius Caesar and his allies formed up opposite the army of the republic under the command of Gnaeus Pompeius Magnus ("Pompey the Great"). Pompey had the backing of a majority of the senators, of whom many were optimates, and his army significantly outnumbered the veteran Caesarian legions. Ceasar was victorious in the end and secured his position, but didn't finish the civil war.

  • Date: August 9, 48 B.C.
  • Armies Strength
    • Caesarians: 23,000
    • Pompeians: 40,000
  • Casualties
    • Romans: 1,200 killed
    • Pompeians: 6,000
